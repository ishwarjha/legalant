# Indian Law Defaults

**Unless explicitly instructed otherwise, all legal reasoning defaults to Indian law and Indian jurisdiction.**

---

## Governing Law Hierarchy

1. Specific statute (e.g., SEBI ICDR 2018 for IPO matters)
2. General statute (e.g., Indian Contract Act 1872 for contracts)
3. Common law principles as adopted by Indian courts
4. International conventions ratified by India (e.g., New York Convention for arbitration)

---

## Core Statutes

| Subject | Statute | Key Sections |
|---------|---------|--------------|
| Contracts | Indian Contract Act 1872 | Sections 10, 14, 23, 55, 73, 74 |
| Property | Transfer of Property Act 1882 | Sections 54, 58, 105, 118 |
| Arbitration | Arbitration & Conciliation Act 1996 (as amended 2015, 2019) | Sections 7, 11, 16, 29A, 34, 36 |
| Limitation | Limitation Act 1963 | Article 113 (contract), Article 137 (residual) |
| Registration | Registration Act 1908 | Section 17 (compulsory registration) |
| Companies | Companies Act 2013 | Sections 77, 117, 179, 186, 188, 230-232 |
| Securities | SEBI ICDR Regulations 2018 | — |
| Listed entities | SEBI LODR Regulations 2015 | Regulation 30 (disclosures) |
| Foreign exchange | FEMA 1999 | Section 6 (capital account), FEMA 20(R) |
| Data protection | DPDP Act 2023 | Sections 7, 9, 16 |
| Labour | ID Act 1947 / Labour Codes 2020 | — |

## Limitation Periods (Key)

| Matter Type | Period | Starts From |
|-------------|--------|-------------|
| Suit on contract | 3 years | Date of breach |
| Suit for money | 3 years | Date money due |
| Suit on judgment | 12 years | Date of decree |
| Arbitration claim | 3 years | Date of cause of action |
| Section 34 challenge | 3 months + 30 days | Date of award |

---

## FEMA Defaults

- Singapore / foreign counterparty = **Person Resident Outside India (PROI)**
- FDI from Singapore via SIA (Automatic Route) unless sector restricted
- Downstream investment rules: Section 6 + FEMA 20(R)
- Pricing guidelines: FEMA 20(R) Rule 21 (fair market value for unlisted shares)

---

## Arbitration Defaults

- Default seat: Mumbai (if not specified in contract)
- Default institutional rules: MCIA (if Indian parties) / ICC (if foreign party)
- Section 29A timeline: 12 months from constitution of tribunal; 18-month outer limit
- Section 34 challenge: 3 months from date of award (extendable by 30 days only)
