# Contract Basics Skill

## Purpose
Guide agents reviewing contracts under Indian law. Apply to all contract review tasks.

## Key Review Checklist

### 1. Parties & Capacity
- Identify all parties: legal name, registered address, entity type (Company/LLP/Individual/Trust)
- Verify signatories have authority — board resolution required for companies under Companies Act, 2013
- Check for power of attorney if signing through a representative
- Confirm company incorporation status via MCA if in doubt

### 2. Definitions Clause
- Flag defined terms used inconsistently across the document
- Note definitions that are overbroad, circular, or internally contradictory
- Verify all cross-references to defined terms are accurate
- Check: every capitalised term used in the body must be defined, and every definition must be used

### 3. Core Obligations
- Extract each party's obligations in a table: Party | Obligation | Deadline | Consequence of Breach
- Identify conditions precedent vs. conditions subsequent
- Note obligations that are absolute vs. best-efforts vs. reasonable-efforts (different standards)

### 4. Payment Terms (Indian Context)
- Check FEMA compliance if cross-border payments are involved (Foreign Exchange Management Act, 1999)
- Note TDS deduction obligations (Income Tax Act, 1961, s. 194C/194J as applicable)
- Check GST applicability (CGST Act, 2017) and correct rate/HSN code
- Verify payment milestones are tied to specific, measurable deliverables
- Indian numbering: use lakh/crore notation; spell out amounts on first use

### 5. Intellectual Property
- Who owns IP created under the contract? (Contractor-created vs. client-owned)
- Work-for-hire vs. assignment vs. licence — distinguish clearly
- Background IP vs. foreground IP — define and protect background IP
- Are moral rights addressed? (Copyright Act, 1957, s. 57)

### 6. Confidentiality
- Duration of obligation (including post-termination survival period)
- Standard carve-outs: publicly available, independently developed, received from third party, required by law/court
- Obligation to notify the other party on unauthorized disclosure
- Permitted disclosures: affiliates, advisors, employees on need-to-know

### 7. Limitation of Liability
- Cap amount — check if reasonable relative to contract value
- Excluded losses: indirect, consequential, special, loss of profit, loss of revenue
- Carve-outs from cap: fraud, wilful misconduct, IP indemnity, data breach, death/personal injury
- Indian courts have upheld reasonable limitation of liability clauses (check recent case law)

### 8. Indemnification
- Indemnified events — be specific, avoid open-ended "any breach"
- Indemnification procedure: notice obligation, right to control defence, settlement consent
- Survival period post-termination
- Indemnity vs. damages — note the difference (indemnity does not require proving loss)

### 9. Termination
- Termination for convenience: notice period, payment for work done to date
- Termination for cause: material breach definition + cure period (typically 30 days)
- Insolvency/change of control as termination triggers
- Consequences: survival of clauses, return/destruction of materials, final payment, IP reversion

### 10. Dispute Resolution
- Internal escalation: senior management discussion before formal proceedings
- Arbitration (preferred for commercial disputes):
  - Seat of arbitration (determines procedural law — India or foreign)
  - Venue (physical location of hearings — distinct from seat in Indian law post-Bharat Aluminium)
  - Rules: ICIDR, LCIA, ICC, SIAC, or ad hoc (Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996)
  - Number of arbitrators (1 or 3)
- Court jurisdiction for interim relief under s. 9 of the A&C Act, 1996

### 11. Governing Law & Jurisdiction
- Specify Indian law or foreign law explicitly
- If foreign law: check if Indian courts will enforce (Indian Contract Act principles may still apply)
- Exclusive vs. non-exclusive jurisdiction clause — be deliberate

### 12. Force Majeure
- Qualifying events (include pandemic, regulatory change, acts of government, natural disaster)
- Notice requirement on occurrence (typically 7–14 days)
- Duration before termination right arises (typically 90–180 days)
- Payment obligations during force majeure period

### 13. Boilerplate — Do Not Ignore
- Entire agreement / merger clause: supersedes all prior negotiations
- Amendment procedure: written + signed by authorised signatories
- Non-waiver clause: exercise of right on one occasion does not waive future exercise
- Severability: invalid clause severed, rest of contract survives
- Notices: physical address, email, deemed delivery timelines, IST time zone
- Counterparts + electronic execution: valid under IT Act, 2000 and e-Sign Rules

## Output Format

Always produce:
1. **Executive Summary** (3–5 bullets, plain English, board-level language)
2. **Issues Table**: Issue | Clause No. | Severity | Recommended Fix
3. **Redline Suggestions** (if requested): Original Text → Suggested Revision + Rationale
4. **Open Points List**: Items requiring client instruction before finalising

## Severity Definitions
- **Critical**: Unenforceable clause, missing essential term, regulatory non-compliance, deal-breaker risk
- **High**: Significant commercial risk, one-sided clause, missing key protection
- **Medium**: Negotiating point, clarification needed, drafting ambiguity
- **Low**: Drafting improvement, minor inconsistency, style issue
