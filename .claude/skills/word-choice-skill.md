# Word Choice Skill for Indian Legal Drafting

## Core Principle
Use precise legal language. Avoid ambiguity. Every word must earn its place. Prefer modern plain-English drafting over archaic legalese, while retaining technical precision.

---

## Preferred vs. Avoid

| Avoid | Prefer | Reason |
|-------|--------|--------|
| "shall" (for future tense) | "will" (future fact) | "shall" = obligation only |
| "and/or" | "and" or "or" (pick one) | Grammatically imprecise; courts struggle to interpret |
| "hereinabove" / "hereinafter" | "above" / "below" or named reference | Archaic |
| "party of the first part" | Party name or defined shorthand | Clarity |
| "notwithstanding anything contained herein" | "Despite anything else in this Agreement" | Modern, clear |
| "in the event that" | "if" | Simpler |
| "prior to" | "before" | Simpler |
| "subsequent to" | "after" | Simpler |
| "utilize" | "use" | Simpler |
| "commence" | "begin" or "start" | Simpler |
| "endeavour to" | "use reasonable efforts to" | More precise standard |
| "as soon as possible" | "within [X] Business Days" | Enforceable timeline |
| "best endeavours" | "best efforts" (or define it) | Courts interpret differently |
| "mutually agreed" | "agreed in writing by both Parties" | Specifies form of agreement |
| "forthwith" | "immediately" or "within [X] days" | Archaic; define the period |
| "in witness whereof" | "Signed by the authorised representatives of the Parties" | Modern execution block |

## Indian Law Specific Terminology

### Companies Act, 2013
- "Board" = Board of Directors (always capitalise when referring to a specific board)
- "Director" vs. "Officer" — use "Director" unless the person is specifically an officer as defined
- "Whole-time Director" (WTD), not "Executive Director" unless defined in Articles
- "Key Managerial Personnel" (KMP) — defined term under s. 2(51): CEO, CFO, CS, WTD, MD
- "Promoter" — as defined under SEBI (ICDR) Regulations, 2018 or Companies Act, s. 2(69)
- "Related Party Transaction" — defined under s. 2(76); always check Audit Committee approval requirement
- "Material Subsidiary" — as defined under SEBI (LODR) Regulations, 2015

### Indian Contract Act, 1872
- "Promisor" and "Promisee" — use when citing the Act; use Party names in contract body
- "Consideration" — must be lawful; past consideration is valid in India (unlike English law)
- "Void" — has no legal effect at all (e.g., agreement in restraint of trade under s. 27)
- "Voidable" — valid until avoided by the innocent party (e.g., contract induced by misrepresentation)
- "Specific Performance" — available under Specific Relief Act, 1963 (as amended 2018); no longer discretionary for infrastructure contracts

### Property Law
- "Immovable property" — use this term in legal instruments, not "real estate" or "real property"
- "Encumbrance" — mortgages, charges, liens, easements collectively
- "Registered instrument" — requires registration under Registration Act, 1908 for immovable property transfers
- "Stamp duty" — state-specific; always specify which state's stamp act applies
- "Lessee" and "Lessor" (not "tenant" and "landlord" in formal instruments)

### FEMA / RBI
- "Authorised Dealer" (AD) Bank — bank authorised to deal in foreign exchange
- "Overseas Direct Investment" (ODI) — Indian resident investing abroad
- "Foreign Direct Investment" (FDI) — foreign entity investing in India
- "Compounding" — RBI's process for rectifying FEMA violations

### Arbitration (A&C Act, 1996)
- "Seat" of arbitration — determines applicable procedural law (landmark: Bharat Aluminium v. Kaiser Aluminium)
- "Venue" — physical location of hearings; different from seat
- "Arbitral Tribunal" (not "arbitral panel" or "arbitration panel")
- "Award" (not "judgment" or "verdict" — those are court terms)
- "Arbitral proceedings" (not "arbitration proceedings")

## Obligation Words — Use Precisely

| Word | Meaning | Use When |
|------|---------|----------|
| **shall** | Binding obligation | Imposing a duty on a party |
| **may** | Discretionary right | Conferring an option, not an obligation |
| **must** | Requirement/condition | Conditions precedent, compliance requirements |
| **shall not** | Prohibition | Restricting a party's action |
| **will** | Future fact | Describing what will happen, not imposing duty |
| **is entitled to** | Right that may be exercised | Conferring a right without imposing obligation |
| **agrees to** | Contractual commitment | Equivalent to "shall" — use consistently |

## Numbers, Dates, and Currency

### Currency
- First use: "Indian Rupees Ten Lakh (₹10,00,000)"
- Subsequent use: "₹10,00,000" or "the Fee"
- Indian numbering: lakh (1,00,000) and crore (1,00,00,000) — not million/billion in Indian instruments
- Foreign currency: "United States Dollars One Hundred Thousand (USD 100,000)"
- Never write "$" alone — specify USD, AUD, SGD, etc.

### Dates
- Format: 15 August 2024 (day Month YYYY — no ordinal, no comma)
- Time: "5:00 p.m. IST" — always specify IST for Indian contracts
- Avoid: "within a reasonable time" — specify exact days/Business Days
- Define "Business Days": working days in [city], excluding public holidays under [state] law

### Defined Terms
- Capitalise all defined terms consistently throughout the document
- Define on first use: `ABC Private Limited ("**Company**")` — bold the shorthand
- Do not use a defined term before it is defined
- Add an Interpretation clause: "References to a Party include its successors and permitted assigns"
- Singular includes plural and vice versa unless context requires otherwise

### Lists and Sub-clauses
- Use `(a), (b), (c)` for sub-clauses; `(i), (ii), (iii)` for sub-sub-clauses
- End each item with a semicolon; "and" or "or" before the final item
- "and" = all items must be satisfied (conjunctive — more restrictive)
- "or" = any one item sufficient (disjunctive — less restrictive)
- "including" = non-exhaustive; add "but not limited to" if needed, or define as "including without limitation"

## Common Drafting Errors to Flag

1. **Ambiguous pronoun reference**: "The Company shall notify the Client when it receives the payment" — who is "it"?
2. **Missing subject**: "Shall be liable for all losses arising from…" — which party shall be liable?
3. **Conflicting clauses**: Same topic addressed differently in two separate clauses — flag both, note conflict
4. **Undefined capitalised terms**: Capital letter used without a corresponding definition
5. **Orphaned definitions**: Term defined in the Definitions clause but never used in the body
6. **Missing carve-out**: Absolute obligation that should have standard exceptions (e.g., force majeure)
7. **Time zone omission**: "by 5 PM" without specifying IST — unenforceable in multi-timezone transactions
8. **Currency ambiguity**: "$1,00,000" — which dollar? Always specify
9. **Circular definition**: "Affiliate means a related Affiliate" — meaningless
10. **Unenforceably broad restraint**: Non-compete covering all of India for 5 years — likely void under s. 27 ICA
